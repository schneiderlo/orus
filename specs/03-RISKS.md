# Orus Risk Register

**Status:** Ready  
**Current risk horizon:** M0 delivery, with M1-M11 architectural risks retained
for roadmap planning  
**Last updated:** 2026-07-21

## 1. Scales and operating rule

| Rating | Likelihood definition | Impact definition |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Not expected in the milestone's normal path; credible only under unusual conditions. | Local rework or delay; no false product claim, security boundary failure, milestone redesign, or release block. |
| Medium | Credible during the milestone and likely to occur at least once without mitigation. | Blocks a domain or requires material rework, but the current architecture and release remain recoverable. |
| High | Expected or already inherent without active mitigation. | Can produce false execution evidence, security/data exposure, commercially unusable performance, unreproducible releases, or redesign across milestones. |

Risk owners must execute the validation plan before the mapped milestone gate.
A failed validation either blocks that gate or requires an accepted ADR that
changes the plan without weakening a binding charter decision. “Monitor” alone
is not a mitigation.

## 6. Review cadence

- M0 risk owners review R-001 through R-010 at each domain-spec readiness
  review and again at the M0 gate.
- A later risk becomes gate-blocking when its owning domain spec moves from
  Draft to Ready; mitigation and validation details must be made concrete in
  that domain spec before it becomes Active.
- Any observed risk that can produce false execution evidence, security/data
  exposure, uncontrolled external effects, or unreproducible release evidence
  is an immediate gate stop, regardless of its prior likelihood rating.
- Closed risks remain in milestone evidence with the validating artifact; they
  are not deleted from history.
